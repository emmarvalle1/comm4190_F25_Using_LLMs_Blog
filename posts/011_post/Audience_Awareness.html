Blog Task Overview

What is this blog post doing?

This post tests whether an LLM can recognize audience differences and adapt its communication style accordingly, a core concept in communication studies.

In human communication, effective messaging depends on:

Who the audience is

What prior knowledge they have

What tone and level of detail is appropriate

This blog evaluates whether LLMs can perform this same kind of audience-aware framing, or whether they simply simplify language without truly adapting meaning.

Objective The task in this experiment is to ask the LLM to explain the same concept to three different audiences: a middle school student, a college professor, and a business executive. By holding the topic constant and changing only the audience, this test isolates whether the model can meaningfully adjust its communication style.

Prompt

Explain how artificial intelligence works to: 1. A middle school student 2. A college professor 3. A business executive
